# The City of Limbo

> "Generally, the closer one gets to the Heart, the more protection they receive... and the higher one's standard of living becomes."

## The Bubble
Between the infinite dimensions of the broader universe lies the Void, a space that all interdimensional travelers—from souls on their way to the afterlife to purposeful explorers—must pass through.

Within this Void sits a mysterious construct known as the **Heart**. The Heart generates a recursive, habitable bubble roughly the size of a medium-sized country, complete with an atmosphere, power, and fresh water. This bubble is a trap: space loops back on itself, so anyone who walks in a straight line away from the Heart will eventually find themselves walking back towards it from the opposite side. Once inside, there is no leaving.

Over time, people and scrap collected in this bubble, forming the sprawling, chaotic metropolis known as **The City** or **Limbo**.

## The Spine and the Heart
The City is built around the **Spine**, a towering monolith of twisted, vine-like white stone that spirals around the Heart, which sits in the precise center of the city. The original creators of the Heart are long gone, though some suspect they still hold power within the city's circles.

## Zones of Limbo
Limbo is a chaotic mess of stacked buildings and ramshackle construction. Its structure is a gradient of wealth and safety, generally split into three sections.

### The Depths
The lowest points of Limbo, a complex maze of decaying buildings, sewers, and shantytowns buried under years of new development. Dark, dangerous, and mostly vacant, few dare to explore these unstable corridors.

### The Gutters
Where the supermajority of the population lives. Conditions range from sketchy to downright dangerous, but inhabitants can at least see the sky between the towering buildings.

### The Rises
The tallest megastructures in the city, typically controlled by single, powerful Circles. This is where everyone wants to be. Even the worst apartments here are safer than the Gutters, and the upper levels are downright lavish.

## Technology
Limbo's technology is a hodgepodge of the incredibly advanced and the outright antiquated.

-   **Advanced:** Cloning, cybernetics, teleportation, 3D printing, and holographic imaging are common.
-   **Dated:** Monochrome holographs, CRTs, and LCDs are the primary displays; LEDs are rare. Sodium lamps are common.
-   **Computing:** Computers are chunky and slow by modern Earth standards. The "internet" is a loose collection of local wired networks.

## Artifice and Power
While there is no native "magic" system, many inhabitants bring powers from their original universes. These cannot be taught or transferred. Other sources of power include:

-   **Augmentation:** Mechanical cybernetics and biological mutations are common.
-   **Artifacts:** Powerful objects often fall into Limbo from the Void.

!!! note "Power Scaling"
    At low-to-mid power levels, guns are the weapon of choice. However, firearms don't scale well against the most powerful individuals, who often use unique melee weapons. At the highest end, urban legends speak of individuals destroying entire buildings with a single blow.

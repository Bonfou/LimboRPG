# The Head, The Hand, and The Law

> "Follow those rules, and you'll never see or hear from the Hand."

## The Head
Limbo is controlled by the **Head**, a mysterious council of an estimated 5 to 15 individuals who dwell within the Spine. They rarely interfere in the daily lives of the city's inhabitants, save for enforcing three absolute rules.

!!! quote "The Three Rules"
    1.  Pay your taxes.
    2.  Don't interfere with the operations of the Head, Heart, or Hand.
    3.  **Never** use dimensional Artifice within the City.
## The Hand
The **Hand** is the enforcement arm of the Head. Their primary duties are collecting taxes, registering Circles, and shutting down any power strong enough to threaten the city's stability.
### The Fingers
The Hand's most terrifying enforcers are the **Fingers**. Their identities and numbers are secret, but their power is legendary. Facing a single Finger in combat is considered suicidal for even the most powerful individuals. They average around Anomaly Tier VII.

!!! example "The Eclipse Hour"
    In an event known as the Eclipse Hour, four Fingers massacred the entire leadership of **Orion**, a powerful Circle, in just under three hours.
### Auditors
When a Finger is overkill, the Hand sends an **Auditor**. Rumored to be lab-grown, these humanoid creatures are tall and imposing, averaging around seven feet. They wear pristine white armored trench coats and spherical, black fishbowl helmets with a ring of yellow holographic caution tape encircling it. They fight with a pair of black gauntlets that can emit bursts of raw energy and enhance the Auditor's physical strength. Auditors serve as the main tax collectors in Limbo, and are all Anomaly Tier IV. They're dangerous, but can be brought down by coordinated groups of Tier II and III individuals. The threat, however, isn't the Auditor itself -- it's the infinite number of them the Hand will send, and eventually, the escalation to a Finger.
## Circles
Circles are groups officially recognized by the Hand. They control almost every aspect of day-to-day life in the otherwise lawless city, creating a complex web of tyranny and chaos.

Circles can be anything from businesses to crime syndicates to cults. Registration is mandatory for tax purposes, though many try to avoid it.

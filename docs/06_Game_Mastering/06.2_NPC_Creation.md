# NPC Creation

NPCs in *Limbo* are simplified reflections of Player Characters. They use the same physics (Clashing, EP, Action Slots), but they are built to be run efficiently by the GM.

---

## 1. The Chassis: Tier & Role
To create an NPC, first determine their **Anomaly Tier (AT)** and their **Role**. These two factors dictate their raw stats.

### Step A: Base Stats by Tier
Use this table to set the baseline.

|   Tier   | Base HP | Action Slots | Attribute Range |
| :------: | :-----: | :----------: | :-------------: |
|  **I**   |   10    |      1       |      0 - 2      |
|  **II**  |   20    |      1       |      1 - 3      |
| **III**  |   40    |      2       |      2 - 4      |
|  **IV**  |   60    |      2       |      3 - 5      |
|  **V**   |   80    |      2       |      4 - 6      |
|  **VI**  |   100   |      3       |      5 - 7      |
| **VII+** |  150+   |      3+      |     8 - 10      |

### Step B: The Role Modifier
Apply these modifiers to the Base Stats above.

| Role       | Description                          |   HP Modifier    | Repertoire Limit          |
| :--------- | :----------------------------------- | :--------------: | :------------------------ |
| **Minion** | Fodder, swarms, civilians.           |     **0.5x**     | 1 Off / 1 Def             |
| **Elite**  | Professional soldiers, horrors.      |     **1.0x**     | 2 Off / 2 Def / 1 Passive |
| **Boss**   | Multi-Part entities (See Section 3). | **Sum of Parts** | Custom (Per Part)         |

## 2. The Repertoire
NPCs do not swap Moves. They have a static list.
* **Weapons:** If an NPC carries a weapon, give them 1-2 Standard Moves from that Shell.
* **Custom Moves:** Elites and Bosses should have unique Moves.

---

## 3. Complex Entities (Multi-Part Bosses)
High-threat entities (usually 1 to 2 Tiers above the party) are too complex to be represented by a single health bar. To balance the Action Economy against a full party, these entities are composed of multiple **Parts**.

### 3.1 Anatomy of a Boss
A Boss consists of a **Core** and 1 or more **Parts** (Limbs, Weapons, Tentacles).

* **Action Slots:** The Boss typically has 3 to 4 Action Slots, regardless of how many Parts it has or its Tier. Any Part can use an Action Slot.
* **Shared EP:** The Boss has a single EP pool (Max 6). Any Part can generate or spend EP.
* **Shared Conditions:** Status Effects apply to the **Core**, even if they were applied to a **Part**. If a status effect applies to the Core, it also applies to all the Parts.

### 3.2 Damage and Targeting
Players may target the **Core** or a specific **Part**.

1.  **Damage Transfer:** Any damage dealt to a Part is subtracted from **both** that Part's HP and the Core's Total HP.
2.  **Part Destruction:** When a Part reaches 0 HP, it is **Destroyed**.

### 3.3 Consequences of Destruction
When a Part is Destroyed, the following rules apply immediately:

1.  **Loss of Moves:** The Boss can no longer use Moves associated with that Part.
2.  **Desperation (Optional):** If specified in the Stat Block, the Core may unlock a "Desperation Move" or gain a buff (e.g., *Enraged*) to compensate for the lost limb.

??? note "Example: The Scrap-Titan (Tier V Boss)"
    * **Total HP:** 160
    * **Core (Torso):** 160 HP. Actions: 4. Moves: *Vent Heat* (AOE).
    * **Part A (Saw Arm):** 40 HP. Moves: *Serrated Sweep*.
    * **Part B (Clamp Arm):** 40 HP. Moves: *Hydraulic Crush*.

    *Combat Flow:*
    1.  The Titan starts with **3 Actions** (Core + A + B).
    2.  Players target the **Saw Arm** and deal 40 Damage.
    3.  The Saw Arm is **Destroyed**.
    4.  The Core takes 40 Damage.
    5.  The Titan loses the *Serrated Sweep* move.

## 4. Loot Rules
In *Limbo*, equipment is physical. If an enemy uses it, it is on their body.

### The Drop Rule
* **Weapons:** If an NPC attacks with a Long Gun, they drop a Long Gun Shell (Tier appropriate).
* **Armor:** Armor is usually destroyed during the fight (reduced to Scrap).

### The Lock Rule
To prevent players from looting game-breaking equipment from Bosses, use these tags:
* **Bio-Locked:** The weapon's trigger is coded to the user's DNA. It is useless scrap to anyone else.
* **Artifice-Bound:** The weapon is a manifestation of the user's will. It dissolves into smoke upon their death.
* **Volatile:** The power source is unstable. The item explodes or melts when the user dies.

## 5. Flattening (Demoting Bosses)
You may wish to reuse a Boss model as a "Elite" enemy for higher-tier players later in the campaign. To do this, **Flatten** the stat block.

1.  **Merge HP:** Combine the HP of the Parts into a single pool (usually lowered to the standard Elite multiplier for that Tier).
2.  **Merge Repertoire:** Move the Part-specific moves into the main list.
3.  **Remove Parts:** The entity becomes a single target.

## Narrative Rolling (The Grid)
### Skills, Approaches, and Attributes
All narrative checks are made by referencing the Grid, a 3x3 grid of Attributes (Body, Mind, and Soul) and Approaches (Power, Flow, Resistance). Each Attribute and Approach pair creates a Skill, whose value is the sum of its parents values.

|          |    Power    |    Flow    | Resistance  |
| :------- | :---------: | :--------: | :---------: |
| **BODY** |   `Brawn`   |  `Reflex`  |   `Grit`    |
| **MIND** | `Intellect` |   `Wits`   |   `Focus`   |
| **SOUL** | `Presence`  | `Instinct` | `Willpower` |

- **Brawn** is used when smashing doors down, intimidating someone with a show of force, or dragging something heavy. It's using physical might to get your way.
- **Reflex** is used when jumping between buildings, navigating silently, pickpocketing a keycard. It's using dexterity to get your way.
- **Grit** is used when surviving harsh environments, marching long treks, or withstanding the side effects of drugs. It's using your constitution to get your way.
- **Intellect** is used when attempting to solve a cipher, navigating a maze, or jury-rigging a generator. It's using your raw smarts to get your way.
- **Wits** is used when hacking into a local network, investigating a crime scene, or noticing details others might miss. It's using your mental flexibility to get your way.
- **Focus** is used when sifting through pages of documents, ignoring distractions, or carefully performing a complex task. It's using your mental fortitude to get your way.
- **Presence** is used when inspiring subordinates, terrifying someone, or performing a ballad. It's using your raw force of personality to get your way.
- **Instinct** is used when empathizing with someone, seeing through lies, or interacting with animals. It's using empathy to get your way.
- **Willpower** is used when ignoring a bribe, surviving a psychic attack, or avoiding someone's influence. It's using your emotional fortitude to get your way.

Each Attribute and Approach can go from 0 to 5, and each Skill is the sum of the Attribute and Approach, so they can go from 0 to 10.
### Making The Check
When making a check, choose which Skill you want to roll, and determine its value. Then, roll that many six-sided dice (d6). On a 5 or 6, the die counts as a Beat. In order to cleanly Succeed at a given check, you need at least 2 Beats. If you roll at least one Beat, you earn a Mixed Success -- you succeed, but something else goes wrong. If you roll 0 Beats, you fail.

If you roll more 6s than 1s **and** get a Clean Success, you **Critically Succeed**, meaning something else goes your way, or you succeed spectacularly. Conversely, if you roll more 1s than 6s **and** get a Failure, you **Critically Fail**, meaning you either fail spectacularly or something else goes wrong in addition to failing the check.
### Hard Checks
To mix things up, the GM may sometimes impose **Hard** on a check. This increases all thresholds by 1: a Clean Success is 3 or more Beats, a Mixed Success is 2 Beats, and a Failure is 0 or 1 Beat.

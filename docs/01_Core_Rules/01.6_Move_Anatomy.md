# Move Anatomy

In the City of Limbo, every ability, biological mutation, and tactical maneuver is represented by a **Move Card**. While all cards share a unified design, their colors, icons, and Type Badges identify their role in the game and how they interact with your **Repertoire**.

---

## Identifying a Move
A Move Card is defined by several key components that dictate its cost and effect:

* **Type Badge & Color:** Located at the top-left, the badge (e.g., **OFF**, **UTL**) and the card's border color indicate its category.
* **The Cost:**
    * **EP (Energy Points):** Used for **Fight Moves**. You regenerate 1 EP at the start of your turn and can hold a maximum of 6.
    * **FP (Focus Points):** Used for **Hustle Moves**. These regenerate at the start of a new Chapter and can hold a maximum of 6.
* **Stats:** Combat moves display Dice (:material-dice-6:), Power (:material-lightning-bolt:), and Damage (:fontawesome-solid-burst:).
* **Keywords:** Bolded terms like **Flow**, **Vicious**, or **Innate** that trigger specific rules.

## Fight Moves (The Repertoire)
Fight Moves represent your combat muscle memory. You may only have **8 Fight Moves** equipped in your Repertoire at any time, and they can only be swapped during a **Rest**.

### Offensive Moves
Offensive Moves (<span class="type-badge offensive">OFF</span>) are used during your Allegiance’s Action Phase. Spending an Action Slot allows you to declare one Offensive Move to initiate a **Clash** against a target.

Passive Moves (<span class="type-badge passive">PAS</span>) provide constant combat benefits. Unlike Offensive or Defensive moves, they do not require a Clash to function, but they **do** occupy one of your 8 Repertoire slots.

## Hustle Moves (The Toolkit)
Hustle Moves represent your innate nature and specialized training. You can possess an **infinite number** of Hustle Moves; they never count against your Repertoire limit.

### Utility Moves
Utility Moves (<span class="type-badge utility">UTL</span>) are active abilities used during narrative play. They typically require the expenditure of **Focus Points (FP)** to achieve extraordinary results.

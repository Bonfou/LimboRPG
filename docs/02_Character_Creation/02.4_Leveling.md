# Leveling and Progression
In the City of Limbo, power is not just found; it is built through the accumulation of **Cred**. As you survive encounters and complete objectives for the various Circles of the City, you earn the resources necessary to refine your combat repertoire and enhance your narrative capabilities.

---
## Earning Cred
Progression is split into two distinct tracks. Each time you level up (to a maximum of Level 20), you gain:

* **1 Fight Cred:** Used to purchase or level up **Combat Moves** (Offensive, Defensive, and Passive).
* **1 Hustle Cred:** Used to increase **Attributes/Approaches** or purchase/level up **Utility Moves**.

You begin your career at Level 1 with **2 Fight Cred** and **2 Hustle Cred**.

---
## Fight Progression
Fight Cred is your primary resource for combat specialization. You may spend it in the following ways:
### 1. Unlocking Moves
* **New Move:** Spend 1 Fight Cred to unlock any Move from your Class. That Move starts at Rank 0.
* **Subclass Entry:** Purchasing your first Move from a Subclass tree permanently locks you into that specialization and prevents you from purchasing Moves from other Subclasses.
### 2. Ranking Up Moves
Each Move can be Ranked up 3 times. Each Rank costs 1 Fight Cred.

* When Ranking up a Move, you choose one of two available **Upgrades** (Rank 1, 2, or 3) provided in your Class dossier.
* Once an upgrade is chosen, its effects are permanently added to that Move's card.

---
## Hustle Progression
Hustle Cred allows you to broaden your influence and survivability within the City.

* **The Grid:** Spend 1 Hustle Cred to increase any Attribute (Body, Mind, Soul) or Approach (Power, Flow, Resistance) by 1, up to a maximum of 5.
* **Utility & Traits:** Spend 1 Hustle Cred to unlock a new Utility Move or Trait from your Class list or the [Universal list](../04_Compendium/04.8_Universal_Hustle_Moves).

---
## Repertoire Management
While you can unlock and level many Moves, your **Repertoire**—the Moves you can actually use in a fight—is limited to **8 slots**.

* Only **Fight Moves** (Offensive, Defensive, Passive) occupy these slots.
* You may only swap which Moves are in your Repertoire or rearrange your upgrades during a **Rest**.
* You may only change which Weapons you're wielding during a **Rest**. If you choose to wield fewer Weapon Shells than your Limbs can allow, all remaining Weapon Shells become Unarmed.

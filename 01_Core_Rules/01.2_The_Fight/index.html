    The Fight (Combat)

    Combat in Limbo uses an abstract, turn-based system. To make or survive attacks, you rely on The Clash.

    Allegiances and Turns

    Combatants are divided into Allegiances—groups that share a singular goal (e.g., The Party vs. The Gang).

    • Turn Order: The GM determines the order in which Allegiances act. If there is no clear priority, the players’ Allegiance goes first.
    • The Round: When an Allegiance starts its turn, all members of that Allegiance may act. They may resolve their actions in any order they choose. Once an Allegiance has finished, no member may act again until the next round.

    The Action Economy

    While the Allegiance shares a "Turn," individuals are limited by their Action Slots.

    Action Slots

    Your Anomaly Tier determines how many times you can act in a single round.

    Anomaly Tier Action Slots
    I - II 1
    III - V 2
    VI - VIII 3
    IX - X 4
    • Spending Slots: You may declare one Offensive Move or Action per Slot.
    • Defense is Free: Using a Defensive Move is reactive and does not cost an Action Slot.

    The Repertoire

    You may only bring a limited amount of mental and physical muscle memory into a fight.

    • Limit: You may have a maximum of 8 Moves prepared in your Repertoire at any time.
    • Scope: This includes Offensive Moves, Defensive Moves, and Passives (including those from Genetics or Cybernetics).
    • Swapping: You may reconfigure your Repertoire during a Rest or by visiting a Doctor (for Genetics and Cybernetics).

    Energy Points (EP)

    Moves require energy and concentration.

    • Starting EP: Everyone begins combat with 3 EP.
    • Cap: You can hold a maximum of 6 EP.
    • Regeneration: At the start of your Allegiance's turn, you regenerate 1 EP.
    • Cost: If you have 0 EP, you cannot use Moves that require an EP cost ( EP > 0). You must use Moves with no EP cost or wait to rebuild your pool.

    The Clash

    All combat is resolved through The Clash.

    1. Declaration: The attacker picks a valid Offensive Move and a target.
    2. Response: The target picks a valid Defensive Move.
      • Note: Armor is passive and does not count as a Move. You must select a Defensive Move from your Weapon, Class, or Subclass in order to defend.
    3. Roll: Both parties roll six-sided dice (d6) equal to their Move's Dice stat, and add their Move's Power stat to each die rolled.
    4. Sort: Arrange all dice on each side from highest to lowest to form Pairs.

    Resolution

    Compare the dice Pairwise—highest with highest, 2nd highest with 2nd highest, and so on.

    • Win: If your die is higher than or equal to the opponent's die, you Win the Pair.
      • Effect: Add an instance of the Move's Damage to your total.
    • Loss: If your die is lower than the opponent's die, you suffer a Loss.
      • Effect: You deal no damage. You likely suffer Harm from the opponent's Win.
    • Unopposed: If a die has no counterpart on the opponent's side (one pool is larger than the other), it is an automatic Win.
      • Effect: Add an instance of the Move's Damage to your total.
    • Break: If a die’s modified value is twice that of the opponent's die (e.g., 8 vs 4), it is a Break.
      • Effect: All subsequent dice on the opponent's side are removed from the Clash immediately. The Break itself counts as a standard Win.

    Harm Calculation

    HP loss is referred to as Harm. It is calculated after all dice in a Clash are resolved.

    1. Sum Damage: Sum all Damage from Wins (Opposed and Unopposed).
    2. Apply Scaling: Determine the Tier Disparity between the Attacker (Weapon/Anomaly Tier) and the Target (Armor Tier). Add or subtract the Bonus/Penalty Damage from the Scaling Table to your sum. This is your Gross Harm.
      • Note: If you score 0 Wins, you deal 0 Harm. Scaling bonuses never apply to a failed Clash.
    3. Subtract AV: Subtract the target's total Armor Value (AV) (Base AV + Armor Quality) from the Gross Harm.
    4. Apply Harm: Apply the remaining amount to the target's HP. This is the Net Harm.
      • Note: True Harm cannot be mitigated or avoided.

    Harm Versus Damage

    Harm is a number subtracted from a target's HP. Damage is a stat on Moves used to calculate Harm. If something says "increase Damage," that means increase the DMG stat.

    Examples

    Example 1: Wins and Scaling (Peer)
    • Roland (Tier III) attacks with a Class Move: 4 / 1 / 6
    • Red Guard (Tier III) defends with a Defensive Move: 3 / 0 / 0
    • The Stats: Roland is Tier III. The Guard is Tier III (Disparity 0). The Guard is wearing Medium Armor (Base 2 AV).
    • The Roll:
      • Roland: 5, 3, 1 (Add +1 PWR) 6, 4, 2
      • Guard: 4, 4, 3 (Add +0 PWR) 4, 4, 3
    • The Comparison:
      1. 6 vs 4: Roland Win. (+6 Dmg).
      2. 4 vs 4: Roland Win (Ties go to Attacker). (+6 Dmg).
      3. 2 vs 3: Guard Win. (Prevents Damage).
    • The Result:
      • Roland: (12 Harm from Wins) + 0 Scaling (Tier III vs III) = 12 Gross Harm. 12 - 2 AV = 10 Net Harm to Guard.
      • Guard: 1 Win. Prevents 1 instance of damage. 0 Net Harm to Roland.
    Example 2: Breaks and Tier Disparity
    • Amon (Tier IV Weapon / Armor) attacks with a Heavy Weapon: 2 / 4 / 8
    • Thug (Tier II Weapon / Armor) attacks with a Swarm Class Move: 5 / 0 / 2
    • The Stats: Amon has a Tier IV Weapon and the Thug has Tier II Armor (Disparity 2, or +12 Harm to the thug). Amon targets Light Armor (Base 1 AV). Thug targets Heavy Armor (Base 3 AV).
    • The Roll:
      • Amon: 5, 3 (Add +4 PWR) 9, 7
      • Thug: 4, 3, 3, 2, 1 (Add +0 PWR) 4, 3, 3, 2, 1
    • The Comparison:
      1. 9 vs 4: Break (9 > 2x4). Amon Win.
        • Effect: All subsequent Thug dice (3, 3, 2, 1) are removed.
      2. 7 vs N/A: Unopposed. Amon Win.
    • The Result:
      • Amon: (16 Harm from Wins) + 0 Scaling = 16 Gross. 16 - 1 AV = 15 Net Harm to Thug.
      • Thug: 0 Wins. 0 Net Harm to Amon.

    Phases of Combat

    Combat is split into distinct phases to help adjudicate rules. There are two distinct cycles: the macro cycle (the Allegiance turn) and the micro cycle (the Clash sequence).

    Allegiance Turn

    Phase 1: The Upkeep Phase
    • All members of the Allegiance gain 1 EP.
    • Then, all "Start of Turn" effects trigger. If there are conflicting triggers, the active Allegiance decides the order.
    Phase 2: The Action Phase
    • The free-flow main phase. Any members of the Allegiance can take Actions in any order, including interweaving them between members (character A acts, character B acts, then character A acts again).
    • This is where EP and Action Slots are spent, Moves are activated, and Actions taken.
    Phase 3: The Cleanup Phase
    • All "End of Turn" effects (such as Bleed's Harm) trigger. If there are conflicting triggers, the active Allegiance decides the order.
    • All statuses decay by 3.
    • Once this Phase ends, initiative is passed to the next Allegiance.

    Clash Sequence

    Step 1: Targeting Step

    The following occur in order:

    • 1.1 Declaration: the attacker declares an Offensive Move, pays the EP cost, then declares a target. If the EP cost cannot be paid, the Move cannot be activated. When declaring an Offensive Move that is not Innate, you must choose which of your equipped Weapons you are using to make that Move. Only the effects of that Weapon's Feature are applied.
    • 1.2 Intervention: this is the short window where the target may be changed. The target may be changed multiple times.
    • 1.3 Lock-In: the final target is locked in.
    • 1.4 Defense: the final target declares a Defensive Move and pays the EP cost. If the EP cost cannot be paid, the Move cannot be activated. If no Defensive Moves can be activated, the defender rolls 0 dice and all the attacker's dice are considered Unopposed.
    Step 2: Rolling Step

    The following occur in order:

    • 2.1 Rolling: all dice ( DIE) are rolled simultaneously.
    • 2.2 Manipulation: all dice manipulation abilities happen (re-rolls, changing values, etc.)
    • 2.3 Power Addition: Power ( PWR) is added to each.
    • 2.4 Arrangement: All dice on each side are arranged from highest to lowest and paired off.
    • 2.5 Calculation: Pairs are evaluated one by one from highest to lowest. If a Break occurs, all subsequent dice on the opposing side are removed before they can be evaluated. Each Win adds an instance of the Move's Harm to its target's Harm Pool.
    Step 3: Application Step

    The following occur in order:

    • 3.1 Gross Harm Modification: The Harm Pool (Gross Harm) is modified by AV, Anomaly Tier scaling (if Gross Harm from Wins is greater than 0), and any reductions/additions. Multipliers are added from highest to lowest, with positive multipliers being applied first.
    • 3.2 Net Harm Application: The finalized Harm Pool (Net Harm) is subtracted from each target's HP.
    • 3.3 Keyword and Status Application: All status effects inflicted by the Move are applied. All "On Win," "On Break," and "On Loss" triggers are applied.

    Status Effects and Conditions

    Statuses

    Status Effects in Limbo stack, and usually have two effects: a triggered effect, which typically deals True Harm equal to stacks, and a passive effect that doesn't scale and applies for as long as an entity has stacks.

    For example, Tremor deals True Harm equal to stacks when the afflicted target is Broken, and while the target has any stacks of Tremor, they get a non-scaling -2 AV penalty.

    Status Decay

    Statuses decay by 3 stacks at the end of the afflicted target's Allegiance turn, to a minimum of 0.

    Conditions

    Conditions are binary states. You either have one or you don't. They're usually only dispelled by certain circumstances, such as healing above a threshold (Bloodied, Mortal) or attacking/getting hit (Hidden).

    Anomaly Tier Reference Table

    Everything falls somewhere on this table, from speed to strength to social influence. It's used as a benchmark for many out-of-combat Moves and Features. Player characters also have an Anomaly Tier, though this caps at VII.

    Tier Designation Scope (Size / Range) Social (Influence / Cost) Narrative Benchmark
    I Unknown Human-sized / Same location One person / Free Throwing a rock; Walking speed; Unarmored.
    II Acolyte Tall Creature / Touch Small Group / Pocket change A pistol shot; An Olympic athlete; Padded leather.
    III Vigilance Large Animal / Small Room Local Celebrity / Low cost A rifle shot; Fast animal; Kevlar vest.
    IV Warning Huge Animal / Large Room Gang Leader / Common purchase Lightning strike; Average vehicle; Heavy armor.
    V Oracle Small Building / Entire Building District Commander / Expensive Small explosion; Fast vehicle; Armored transport.
    VI Tyrant Large Building / City Block City Council / Unaffordable Large explosion; Airplane speed; Bank vault.
    VII Apex Hundreds of feet / District Large Circle / Circle Treasury Building collapse; Supersonic; Bunker.
    VIII Eclipse Miles / Several Districts The Hand / Head Treasury Nuke; Faster than sight; Invulnerable.
    IX City A Country / The City The Head / All Wealth Planet-buster; Instant travel; Functionally Invincible.
    X Heart Cosmic / Dimension A Law of Nature / Incalculable Black hole; Acausal movement; Universal truth.

    The Cycle of Play

    Time in Limbo RPG is tracked by Scenes and Chapters.

    Scenes

    Scenes are individual vignettes. A Scene is a single encounter or challenge, and is often self-contained. Examples of Scenes include:

    • Talking to a shopkeeper.
    • Getting into a fight.
    • Bypassing a locked door.

    Typically, if the GM establishes a new environment or situation, or begins or ends combat, that's a new Scene.

    Chapters

    Chapters are collections of Scenes. Rather than being a single gameplay session, Chapters can be less than one, or multiple (though more often than not they're at least one full session, if not more). A Chapter is a self-contained arc that begins and ends at a "breakpoint" in the story. Examples of Chapters include:

    • Breaking someone out of prison.
    • Attending a party in the Rises.
    • Assassinating a target.

    Typically, if the GM establishes a new primary objective, that's a new Chapter.

    Focus Points (FP)

    Focus Points are used to power certain Utility Moves and to Push Checks. They regenerate fully at the start of a new Chapter, up to their limit (6).

    Narrative Rolling (The Grid)

    Skills, Approaches, and Attributes

    All narrative checks are made by referencing the Grid, a 3x3 grid of Attributes (Body, Mind, and Soul) and Approaches (Power, Flow, Resistance). Each Attribute and Approach pair creates a Skill, whose value is the sum of its parents values.

    Power Flow Resistance
    BODY Brawn Reflex Grit
    MIND Intellect Wits Focus
    SOUL Presence Instinct Willpower
    • Brawn is used when smashing doors down, intimidating someone with a show of force, or dragging something heavy. It's using physical might to get your way.
    • Reflex is used when jumping between buildings, navigating silently, pickpocketing a keycard. It's using dexterity to get your way.
    • Grit is used when surviving harsh environments, marching long treks, or withstanding the side effects of drugs. It's using your constitution to get your way.
    • Intellect is used when attempting to solve a cipher, navigating a maze, or jury-rigging a generator. It's using your raw smarts to get your way.
    • Wits is used when hacking into a local network, investigating a crime scene, or noticing details others might miss. It's using your mental flexibility to get your way.
    • Focus is used when sifting through pages of documents, ignoring distractions, or carefully performing a complex task. It's using your mental fortitude to get your way.
    • Presence is used when inspiring subordinates, terrifying someone, or performing a ballad. It's using your raw force of personality to get your way.
    • Instinct is used when empathizing with someone, seeing through lies, or interacting with animals. It's using empathy to get your way.
    • Willpower is used when ignoring a bribe, surviving a psychic attack, or avoiding someone's influence. It's using your emotional fortitude to get your way.

    Each Attribute and Approach can go from 0 to 5, and each Skill is the sum of the Attribute and Approach, so they can go from 0 to 10.

    Making The Check

    When making a check, choose which Skill you want to roll, and determine its value. Then, roll that many six-sided dice (d6). On a 4, 5, or 6, the die counts as a Beat. The number of Beats you score is then compared to the Difficulty of the check, which the GM determines. If the number of Beats meets or exceeds the Difficulty, you succeed at what you were trying to do. Otherwise, you fail.

    If you roll more 6s than 1s, you get a Boon, meaning you get an additional positive effect regardless of whether you succeed or fail. Conversely, if you roll more 1s than 6s, you get a Bane, meaning you get an additional negative effect regardless of whether you succeed or fail.

    Difficulty

    The table below is a list of common Difficulties and their narrative descriptions. GMs should choose a Difficulty that best suits the situation. Keep in mind some checks may be impossible to succeed on.

    Difficulty Value Description
    Common 1 Might take some effort for some people.
    Standard 2 Many might fail, many might succeed.
    Challenging 3 Uncertain for most people.
    Hard 4 Challenging even for the skilled.
    Extreme 5 Impossible for most people.
    Superhuman 6 Noticeably beyond the capacity for a normal human.
    Infeasible 7 Outstanding even among superhumans.
    Impossible 8+ Uncertain even for the strongest.

    Pushing Checks

    Checks can be Pushed to grant additional dice. When making a check, you may spend any number of FP to add that many additional dice to your check.

    Assisted Checks

    Other party members can also Assist on checks. Each person assisting must narratively justify how they help, and if they can, the player making the check adds dice equal to 1/2 of each assistant's Skill, rounded down (to a minimum of 1). A player can only be Assisted by up to 2 people at once.

    Resting

    Sometimes, you need to take a breather. When you choose to relax or sleep for at least 8 hours, you are considered Resting. When you Rest, the Scene automatically ends and you regain 12 HP (being Bloodied means you only regain 6, while being Mortal means you do not regain any). While Resting, and only while resting, you may swap weapons and rearrange your Repertoire.
